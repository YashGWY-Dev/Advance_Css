Eternal Wisdom of Shree Krishna

"कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥"
"You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but not to the fruits of action.
Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities,
and never be attached to not doing your duty."
- Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 2, Verse 47
The Profound Meaning
This timeless verse teaches us the essence of Karma Yoga - the path of selfless action. Krishna reminds us that our dharma (righteous duty) is to act with complete dedication and sincerity, but we must release our attachment to the outcomes. When we perform our duties without expecting specific results, we free ourselves from anxiety, disappointment, and ego. This doesn't mean being careless or indifferent, but rather giving our best effort while surrendering the results to the divine will. This principle transforms work into worship, stress into serenity, and ordinary actions into spiritual practice. It's the secret to finding peace and purpose in our daily lives, no matter what challenges we face.
